01

Executive Summary

The project Lumina is a digital solution designed to help local communities safely report incidents of injustice and fear without risking exposure. The app focuses on privacy, trust, and easier access to help, particularly in areas with high corruption and low reporting rates. It prioritizes features like a panic mechanism and anonymous reporting to empower users.

03

Project Final Evaluation

The project aims to measure both quantitative and qualitative impacts:

  • A target 10% increase in user perception of safety from baseline after launch.
  • Minimum 30–50 active users in the first month.
  • Testimonials on users feeling safer to report incidents will indicate success.
04

Impact

Lumina addresses a pressing social issue in the Philippines, where fear of retaliation and low trust in traditional reporting channels discourage citizens from reporting injustice. By offering anonymity and privacy-first design, Lumina intends to make reporting safer and more approachable. This supports safer communities and could contribute to stronger institutions over time.

05

Reflection

Process of Completing Project

Completing the Lumina capstone involved user-centered research, prototyping, testing, and refining features like the panic mechanism and anonymous reporting. The team followed an Agile user-centered design cycle where feedback influenced development at each stage.

Barriers / Obstacles

The team had to balance strong privacy measures with usability, ensuring incident reporting could be done safely and easily. They also had to estimate realistic costs and timing for development, review, and compliance with legal standards.

What Was Learned

Students learned how to design with user safety and privacy in mind, how to create a project timeline and budget, and how to measure impact in quantitative and qualitative ways. They also understood legal frameworks like data privacy laws relevant to tech products.

Relation to Subject

The project directly reflects ICT course objectives: applying technology to solve real-world problems, conducting research, producing a working prototype with documentation, and evaluating its societal impact.
